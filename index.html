Junwen Hu

Hello! How's it going? Pretty good, doing okay, or not as good as it could be? 

I'm a Ph.D. student in Communication at Michigan State University. I'm a communication researcher with an ultimate goal of understanding how communication can be used to make people happy.

I study health communication, mostly with an interpersonal approach. Broadly, I'm also interested in information processing processes on social media. Methodologically, I'm seeking to use computational and psysiological tools to unpack the black box between messages and their effects. In three years, I presented my research at ICA, NCA, etc, for over ten times. 

I believe words can be meaningful actions. "Unhealthy behaviors" like smoking and social media addiction may reflect efforts to cope with real-life stress when alternative resources are limited. By leveraging communication to reduce stress and facilitate reappraisal on their situations, we can induce change towards a desired direction. 

In addition to research, I teach public speaking. I believe students learn best through interaction and that is what I try to facilitate in my class. I also believe in personalizing teaching techniques based on individual differences.
